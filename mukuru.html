Mukuru, Kenya

Understanding the community we serve in Eastern Nairobi.

The Community We Serve

We have been supporting a Christian church and school for 20 years in the slums of Mukuru, located in Nairobi, Kenya.

Nairobi has approximately 2.5 million slum dwellers across 200 settlements, yet 60% of the population occupies only 6% of the land. The Mukuru slums consist of approximately 30 villages and are home to more than 700,000 people.

Life in Mukuru

Life in Nairobi is often described as being separated by a wall. On one side exists a modern lifestyle enjoyed by the middle and upper classes. On the other side are communities where access to decent shelter, toilets, running water, electricity, and sewage systems remains limited.

Only 10% of the population owns their homes, while the remaining 90% are tenants without ownership rights.

Living Conditions

Housing

The average shack measures approximately 12 feet by 12 feet, built from mud and corrugated tin walls and roofs. These structures often house eight or more residents.

Rent

The monthly rent for a slum dwelling is approximately 700 Kenyan shillings, or about $6 USD.

Land & Ownership

Only 10% of the population owns their homes. The remaining 90% are tenants without ownership rights or security of tenure.
